# A Full Week of Summer Meal Prep for Under $75 — Plan + Shopping List Included

Buying individual meals or winging dinner every night costs more than you think — not just in money but in mental energy. A week of meal prep collapses both problems at once. This plan covers 5 lunches and 5 dinners for one to two people, built around summer staples that are cheap right now, and it comes in under $75 at most major grocery stores. Here's exactly what to buy, what to cook on Sunday, and how to assemble it all week without eating the same thing twice.

## The Shopping List (Under $75)

The proteins anchor the week. Two pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs ($7–9), one pound of ground turkey ($6–7), and two cans of chickpeas ($2) cover every meal with room to spare. Chicken thighs over breasts: the fat keeps them moist even after three days in the fridge, and they're at least $2/lb cheaper than boneless breast.

Grains and starches: a 2-lb bag of brown rice ($3), one pound of dry lentils ($2), and a 5-lb bag of potatoes ($5). These are the backbone of every lunch and most dinners.

Summer produce at seasonal lows: a 5-lb bag of zucchini ($4), one large bunch of kale ($3), two pints of cherry tomatoes ($5), one bunch of scallions ($2), two heads of garlic ($2), and two lemons ($2).

Pantry: a 28-oz can of crushed tomatoes ($3), olive oil ($5 if you're out), soy sauce ($3 if needed), cumin, paprika, and dried oregano. Two dozen eggs ($5–6).

**Running total: approximately $63–72 depending on your store.** Leave the rest as a buffer for anything you already have.

## Sunday Prep: 3 Hours, 5 Days of Meals

**Start with the rice first** — it takes the longest and you can ignore it: 2 cups dry brown rice to 4 cups water, bring to a boil, cover, simmer 40 minutes. Don't open the lid.

**While the rice cooks, roast the potatoes and zucchini:** quarter the potatoes, slice the zucchini into half-moons, toss both with olive oil, salt, and a pinch of paprika. Roast at 425°F for 25–30 minutes, flipping once. The zucchini is done a few minutes before the potatoes — pull it when the edges are just beginning to brown and the centers are soft.

**Cook the chicken thighs:** season with cumin, salt, and garlic. Sear in a hot, dry skillet for 5–6 minutes per side until the skin side is deeply golden, then finish in a 400°F oven for 12–15 minutes. Let rest 5 minutes before slicing across the grain — cutting across the muscle fibers makes three-day-old chicken dramatically more pleasant to eat.

**Lentils for lunches:** 1 cup dry lentils to 2.5 cups water, bring to a boil, reduce heat, simmer 20–25 minutes with a pinch of cumin and a bay leaf. Drain, then season with salt, lemon juice, and olive oil while still warm so they absorb the flavors as they cool. These get better on day 2.

**Boil 8 eggs on a free burner:** start in cold water, bring to a boil, 9 minutes for fully set yolks, then immediately into an ice bath. These go on top of lunches and provide quick grab-and-go protein all week.

## The Weekly Meal Matrix

**Lunches (rotate):** Lentil bowls with cherry tomatoes, scallions, and a soft-boiled egg. Or brown rice + roasted zucchini + sliced chicken thigh with lemon squeezed over the top. Neither requires reheating — just let the refrigerated grain come to room temperature for 20 minutes.

**Dinners — Monday and Tuesday:** Sliced chicken thighs over brown rice with roasted vegetables, finished with a quick pan sauce. Deglaze the now-empty skillet with a splash of soy sauce and water, scrape the fond, and pour it over the plate. Two minutes of effort, serious flavor.

**Wednesday:** Ground turkey with canned crushed tomatoes and garlic over rice. This is the quickest dinner of the week — 20 minutes start to finish and zero prep. Brown the turkey, add the garlic, pour in the tomatoes, simmer 10 minutes while you reheat rice.

**Thursday and Friday:** Egg-and-potato hash built from whatever's left — potatoes, roasted zucchini, kale, and eggs all cooked together in one skillet. The technique that matters: get the potatoes hot and slightly crisped before adding the eggs, or the whole thing turns to mush.

## FAQ

**How far in advance can I prep all of this safely?**
Everything here keeps 4–5 days refrigerated in airtight containers — cooked chicken, lentils, rice, and roasted vegetables all hold well from Sunday through Friday. The boiled eggs last a full week unpeeled; peel only what you'll eat in the next two days. Any sour smell or sliminess on the chicken is a clear signal to toss it — don't override your senses with "it's probably fine."

**The lentils and rice start tasting flat by Wednesday. Easy fix?**
Almost always acid. A squeeze of fresh lemon, a splash of apple cider vinegar, or a spoonful of your favorite hot sauce right before eating will completely transform a flat bowl. Add it when you're plating, not during storage — acid breaks down the texture of cooked lentils and makes refrigerated grains mushy if left to soak overnight.

**Can I substitute chicken thighs if I don't eat poultry?**
A 2-lb block of firm tofu ($4–5) works almost 1:1. Press it for 30 minutes under a heavy plate to squeeze out moisture, cube it, season the same way as the chicken, and roast at 400°F for 25–30 minutes until the edges are well-browned and the texture firms up. It absorbs flavors well and holds 4 days in the fridge without going rubbery the way softer tofu does.
